# Emotion Cards — Phase 4 Launch Outreach Execution Plan

**Game:** Emotion Cards  
**Studio:** NorthernStar Studio  
**Owner:** Gabe 📊  
**Branch target:** `release/v0.5.0`  
**Status:** Execution-ready launch plan

---

## Purpose

This document translates the earlier press and creator planning into a **launch execution plan** for the final pre-release and launch window.

The job in Phase 4 is not to "do more outreach." It is to:
- time outreach so press and creators can actually act on it
- convert wishlists into day-one traffic and purchases
- keep messaging consistent across Steam, creators, press, and community
- avoid wasting the launch week on late scrambling
- preserve momentum if the first wave underperforms

**Core positioning to repeat everywhere:**
- Emotion Cards is a **narrative-forward roguelike deckbuilder**
- the distinct hook is **emotion-based strategy with narrative consequences**
- the pitch is **not** "another indie deckbuilder"; it is **the deckbuilder with emotional identity**

---

## Phase 4 Launch Window

This plan assumes a structured launch runway beginning **4 weeks before release** and extending through **2 weeks after launch**.

### Working timeline
- **T-28 to T-21 days:** review/preview seeding starts
- **T-20 to T-14 days:** second outreach wave and creator scheduling
- **T-13 to T-7 days:** reminder wave, wishlist conversion push, asset lock
- **T-6 to T-1 days:** launch confirmation, embargo prep, creator support
- **Launch day:** coordinated comms across Steam, press, creators, and community
- **T+1 to T+14 days:** follow-up, missed-coverage outreach, quote harvesting, fallback beats

If the actual launch date moves, keep the relative timing intact. The sequence matters more than the calendar dates.

---

## Launch Goals

### Primary goals
- secure **day-one creator coverage** from genre-fit channels
- secure **launch-day or launch-week press coverage** from warm and best-fit outlets
- convert accumulated Steam interest into a focused **wishlist-to-launch CTA**
- maintain enough follow-up structure that launch momentum lasts beyond 24 hours

### Secondary goals
- generate strong screenshots, quotes, clips, and social proof for the first 7 days
- create a second chance for coverage from outlets that miss launch day
- reduce risk from low response rates, technical hiccups, or launch-week crowding

---

## Messaging Hierarchy for Launch

Use one primary message and only a few supporting messages.

### Primary launch message
> Emotion Cards is out now — a narrative roguelike deckbuilder where emotions shape both your strategy and the story.

### Supporting messages
- Strategic deckbuilding with a clearer emotional and narrative hook than most genre peers
- Distinctive run-based gameplay for deckbuilder and roguelite audiences
- A story-forward angle for players who want more than pure combat optimization
- Available now on Steam

### Launch CTA priority
1. **Wishlists / notify followers:** convert to launch-day store visits
2. **Buy / play now:** once the game is live
3. **Watch creator coverage / read impressions:** social proof support
4. **Join community / follow updates:** retention and second-wave momentum

Do not overload launch messaging with too many asks at once.

## Creator Outreach Timing

Creator outreach should be staggered by audience fit and content lead time, not blasted in one mass email.

## Creator lane structure

### Lane A — Priority genre-fit creators
Targets: deckbuilder, roguelite, strategy-first creators from the existing list.

**Best timing:** **T-28 to T-21 days**

**Goal:**
- secure early interest
- deliver review/preview build with enough lead time
- identify who is planning day-one or launch-week coverage

**What to send:**
- personalized email
- launch date
- review/preview build access details
- key art, screenshots, trailer, Steam page, press/creator kit
- brief explanation of why Emotion Cards fits their audience
- clear ask: launch-day or launch-week coverage consideration

**Priority examples from current creator list:**
- Retromation
- Wanderbots
- Rhapsody
- Splattercatgaming
- Olexa
- Time to Grind
- NeverNathaniel

### Lane B — Narrative and broader indie creators
Targets: creators who respond more to story, tone, or indie discovery framing.

**Best timing:** **T-21 to T-14 days**

**Goal:**
- widen the audience beyond pure card-battler fans
- secure thoughtful coverage that reinforces the emotional identity angle

**What to emphasize:**
- emotional hook
- character and narrative framing
- accessibility for players who are not only hardcore deckbuilder fans

**Priority examples from current creator list:**
- PlayFrame
- Materwelonz
- ChristopherOdd
- Keith Ballard
- Mapocolops
- Gab Smolders

### Lane C — Launch-week backup and amplification creators
Targets: smaller, faster-turnaround, or more reactive channels.

**Best timing:** **T-7 to T+3 days**

**Goal:**
- fill gaps if the first wave underperforms
- increase coverage density during launch week
- support "hidden gem" discovery if attention builds slowly

**What to send:**
- launch-now email or DM
- concise key request response flow
- strongest social proof available so far, if any
- one-line hook + Steam link + press kit

## Creator timing rules
- **Do not wait until launch day** for top-priority creators
- Give priority creators **2-4 weeks of lead time** where possible
- Stagger contacts over several days to manage replies and support cleanly
- Track who requested keys, who redeemed, who scheduled coverage, and who passed
- For creators who prefer DMs, use DM only as the opener, then move to email or key handoff

## Press Outreach Timing

Press outreach should run as a tighter, more selective campaign than creator outreach.

## Press lane structure

### Press Wave 1 — Review and launch consideration
**Timing:** **T-28 to T-21 days**

**Audience:**
- best-fit indie and PC outlets
- warm contacts
- reviewers or writers who already cover deckbuilders, roguelites, or narrative indies

**Goal:**
- secure review consideration
- secure launch-day news or launch-week feature planning
- confirm who wants code and who only wants press materials

**What to send:**
- personalized review request or launch preview email
- launch date and embargo timing
- review build/code instructions
- fact sheet, screenshots, trailer, Steam page, press kit
- support contact for issues/questions

**Priority outlet types:**
- PC and strategy outlets
- indie-specialist publications
- writers with deckbuilder or story-rich indie coverage history

### Press Wave 2 — Secondary and news desk send
**Timing:** **T-14 to T-10 days**

**Audience:**
- secondary outlets
- broader news desks
- launch roundup and indie discovery editors

**Goal:**
- widen launch-week article potential
- capture outlets that do not need long review lead time

**What to send:**
- concise launch-date pitch
- clear assets and Steam page links
- optional interview availability if useful

### Press Wave 3 — Launch-day release note
**Timing:** **Launch day, ideally within the first release window block**

**Audience:**
- full relevant press list
- anyone who previously expressed interest
- anyone who asked to be updated at launch

**Goal:**
- convert awareness into actual coverage
- remind outlets the game is live and link-ready

**What to send:**
- "Emotion Cards is out now" note
- live Steam link
- launch trailer
- review guide / fact sheet / press kit
- any early social proof that is real and not inflated

## Press timing rules
- Reviews need **10-14 days minimum**, preferably longer
- Broad news desks can be contacted later, but not as a substitute for review lead time
- One precise follow-up beats multiple nudges
- If a writer passed or did not engage after the follow-up, downgrade rather than chase

## Wishlist and Launch CTA Sequencing

Wishlists should be treated as the bridge between awareness and launch-day conversion.

## Sequence

### Stage 1 — Final pre-launch buildup
**Timing:** **T-14 to T-7 days**

**Primary CTA:**
- **Wishlist now**
- **Be ready for launch**

**Main channels:**
- Steam page traffic from creator and press links
- social posts
- any newsletter or community updates

**Message focus:**
- release date is fixed
- the store page is ready
- this is the last clean push to increase wishlists before conversion starts

### Stage 2 — Final countdown
**Timing:** **T-6 to T-1 days**

**Primary CTA:**
- **Wishlist / turn attention to launch time**

**Secondary CTA:**
- watch for creator coverage and launch trailer

**Message focus:**
- remind players the game launches soon
- reinforce the hook in one sentence
- avoid long feature dumps; this is about readiness and anticipation

### Stage 3 — Launch day conversion
**Timing:** **Launch day**

**Primary CTA:**
- **Out now on Steam**

**Secondary CTA:**
- watch launch coverage
- share impressions
- join the community if they buy and play

**Important shift:**
Once the game is live, stop leading with wishlist language in public-facing launch messages. The CTA becomes **buy/play now**.

### Stage 4 — Launch week social proof
**Timing:** **T+1 to T+7 days**

**Primary CTA:**
- **Now available**

**Supporting proof:**
- creator videos/live streams
- press quotes
- community reactions
- useful update notes if needed

**Goal:**
- catch people who ignored the countdown but respond to proof and recommendations

## Follow-Up Schedule

This should be followed with discipline. Most outreach fails because teams either never follow up or panic-follow up too often.

## Creator follow-up cadence

### First touch
**When:** T-28 to T-14 depending on creator lane

### Follow-up 1
**When:** **3-5 business days later**

**Use when:**
- no response
- creator is still relevant to the launch plan
- there is a real reason to re-surface the note

**What to include:**
- short reminder
- one-sentence fit note
- current build/key availability
- no guilt language

### Follow-up 2
**When:** **Only if they previously engaged**, asked for timing, or opened a conversation

**When to send:**
- around **T-7 to T-3 days** for scheduling confirmation
- or **T+1 to T+3 days** if they asked to be reminded when live

**Rule:**
No repeated cold follow-ups beyond the first nudge.

## Press follow-up cadence

### First touch
**When:** T-28 to T-10 depending on outlet type

### Follow-up 1
**When:** **4-5 business days later**

**Use when:**
- the outlet is still a meaningful target
- no response yet
- coverage timing still makes sense

### Reminder to engaged contacts
**When:** **T-7 to T-3 days**

**Audience:**
- confirmed reviewers
- writers who asked for code or assets
- editors who said to re-send near launch

**What to include:**
- embargo reminder if applicable
- any build notes
- support contact

### Launch-day update
**When:** launch day

**Audience:**
- everyone who asked to be updated at launch
- relevant press list

### Missed-launch follow-up
**When:** **T+5 to T+7 days**

**Audience:**
- outlets that did not publish but remain a fit
- creators/outlets that are slower-moving

**Angle:**
- in case you missed it
- live now
- early response or community reaction if meaningful

## Weekly Execution Breakdown

## T-28 to T-21 days
- finalize launch date, price, embargo, and key inventory
- lock creator and press target lists into priority tiers
- send priority creator wave
- send first review/press wave
- confirm press kit is current and not carrying outdated assets
- start reply tracking sheet for status: sent, replied, key sent, coverage planned, declined, no response

## T-20 to T-14 days
- send narrative/broader creator wave
- send secondary press wave
- process key requests quickly
- answer build questions and troubleshoot installs/redemptions
- start first follow-up pass for early non-responders
- align public-facing countdown messaging with Steam page

## T-13 to T-7 days
- send reminder notes to engaged contacts
- post final wishlist-focused community messages
- prepare launch-day email drafts, social copy, and community posts
- verify all links, assets, pricing, and timestamps
- identify any obvious coverage gaps and prepare backup creator list

## T-6 to T-1 days
- confirm launch-day creator schedules where possible
- finalize launch trailer and live store assets
- freeze messaging so public-facing copy stays consistent
- prep support roles for launch day: who answers creator questions, who monitors store, who handles press replies
- stop broad cold outreach unless there is a clear strategic reason

## Launch day
- send launch-day press note
- send launch-day creator reminder to opted-in contacts
- publish official live messages across owned channels
- confirm Steam page, price, discount, trailer, screenshots, and tags are correct
- monitor and respond to coverage, quote opportunities, and urgent issues

## T+1 to T+14 days
- collect creator links, article links, and quotes
- send thank-you notes where appropriate
- run missed-launch outreach to slower outlets/creators
- package any real traction into a second-wave social proof beat
- decide whether to shift into update-led or "hidden gem" positioning

## Launch Day Communication Checklist

Use this as the operational checklist for release day.

## Before launch goes live
- [ ] Confirm Steam page is live or set to go live at the correct time
- [ ] Confirm price and launch discount are correct
- [ ] Confirm trailer, screenshots, short description, and capsules are current
- [ ] Confirm store CTA language and launch copy are final
- [ ] Confirm press kit links work
- [ ] Confirm review/creator keys are still redeemable
- [ ] Confirm launch email templates are loaded and ready
- [ ] Confirm support contact is available for press/creator questions
- [ ] Confirm community post copy is approved and consistent

## At launch
- [ ] Publish/store-check the live Steam page immediately
- [ ] Send launch-day press email
- [ ] Send launch-day creator reminder/update to confirmed contacts
- [ ] Publish owned-channel launch posts
- [ ] Share trailer/live link in community spaces
- [ ] Monitor replies, key issues, link problems, and store errors in real time
- [ ] Capture first coverage links and notable reactions

## First 6 hours after launch
- [ ] Verify no broken store assets or pricing mistakes
- [ ] Repost or amplify creator coverage that goes live
- [ ] Reply quickly to press or creator questions
- [ ] Collect accurate public-facing quotes only from real coverage
- [ ] Watch for repeated player confusion points that may require updated messaging

## End of launch day
- [ ] Log all coverage outcomes and pending follow-ups
- [ ] Send thank-you notes to major first-wave supporters where appropriate
- [ ] Prepare next-day recap and social proof posts
- [ ] Decide whether fallback outreach needs to activate on T+1

## Risk Notes and Fallback Moves

The launch plan should assume that at least one thing goes wrong. The point is to recover fast, not pretend it will all be clean.

## Risk 1 — Low creator response rate
**Likely cause:** crowded release window, weak subject lines, unclear hook, late outreach, or mismatch between game and creator audience.

**Fallback moves:**
- shift faster to mid-size genre-fit creators with better response odds
- tighten the ask to "launch-week coverage" instead of strict launch-day coverage
- lead harder with the emotional-strategy differentiator instead of generic deckbuilder framing
- use a small backup DM pass for creators who prefer social-first contact
- amplify every real early creator signal to improve later conversion

## Risk 2 — Low press pickup
**Likely cause:** launch week noise, insufficient review lead time, or story angle not sharp enough.

**Fallback moves:**
- pivot from broad review ask to sharper story angles:
  - narrative deckbuilder angle
  - emotional strategy angle
  - indie hidden gem angle
- focus on indie-specialist and PC-specific outlets rather than chasing giant desks late
- send a clean T+5 to T+7 "in case you missed it" note with live link and any genuine reception proof
- offer interview/commentary angle if a pure review hook is not landing

## Risk 3 — Wishlist conversion underperforms
**Likely cause:** wishlists accumulated without enough final urgency, launch messaging too soft, or Steam page not converting.

**Fallback moves:**
- tighten launch-day messaging to a single clear CTA: **out now on Steam**
- use creator clips, quotes, and strongest GIFs to support conversion posts
- review store-page top section for clarity: hook, screenshots, trailer, first lines of copy
- push launch-week social proof rather than repeating countdown language after release

## Risk 4 — Late build issues or review friction
**Likely cause:** unstable review build, key redemption issues, unclear instructions.

**Fallback moves:**
- send immediate support note to affected contacts
- provide known-issues summary and workaround if applicable
- prioritize creators/outlets still able to cover on launch week rather than insisting on exact launch day
- if needed, frame outreach honestly as launch-week coverage instead of review-at-embargo

## Risk 5 — Crowded release window / bigger game steals attention
**Likely cause:** bad luck and scheduling overlap.

**Fallback moves:**
- lean into differentiated positioning instead of volume
- focus on warm/fit contacts rather than trying to outshout larger launches
- prepare T+3 to T+10 discovery messaging: "if you missed Emotion Cards at launch"
- treat second-week outreach as a valid recovery beat, not a failure state

## Risk 6 — Messaging drift across channels
**Likely cause:** too many people writing copy without one approved hierarchy.

**Fallback moves:**
- lock one-line pitch and launch CTA before T-7
- assign one owner to approve outgoing launch copy
- use the same live Steam link, hook, and proof points everywhere
- remove outdated lines such as wishlist-first language after the game is live

## Recommended Fallback Beats After Launch

If launch day is quiet or mixed, use one of these beats instead of repeating the same announcement.

### Beat A — Hidden gem / discovery push
**Best timing:** T+5 to T+10

**Use when:** coverage exists but awareness is thin.

**Angle:**
- Emotion Cards is live and worth a second look for deckbuilder and narrative indie players

### Beat B — Creator reaction montage / social proof beat
**Best timing:** T+3 to T+7

**Use when:** several creators cover the game but press is light.

**Angle:**
- compile real reactions, clips, quotes, and Steam page link

### Beat C — Post-launch update / responsiveness beat
**Best timing:** T+7 to T+14

**Use when:** players surface issues or feedback quickly.

**Angle:**
- the team is responsive, improving the game, and supporting players immediately

### Beat D — Interview / design story beat
**Best timing:** T+7 onward

**Use when:** the game’s design concept earns curiosity even if reviews lag.

**Angle:**
- why build a deckbuilder around emotions and narrative consequence

## Minimum Operational Assets Needed Before This Plan Starts

This plan only works if the asset package is actually ready.

- stable launch or review build
- final launch date and time
- Steam page live and accurate
- launch trailer
- current screenshots and key art
- fact sheet / press kit / creator kit
- key distribution process
- contact tracking sheet or CRM status list
- approved launch-day messaging templates
- known support owner for press and creators

If any of those are missing, reduce scope and prioritize fewer, better contacts rather than pretending full coverage is still realistic.

---

## Final Recommendation

For Phase 4, NorthernStar should run launch outreach in **three disciplined waves**:
- **early review and priority creator seeding**
- **mid-window reminder and wishlist conversion push**
- **launch-day plus launch-week follow-through**

The most important execution rule is simple:

**Do not confuse noise with timing.**

Emotion Cards will perform better with a smaller number of well-timed, audience-fit touches than with a last-minute volume blast. If the team stays consistent on hook, CTA, and follow-up discipline, this plan gives the game its best shot at a credible launch week and a recoverable second wave if day one is soft.
