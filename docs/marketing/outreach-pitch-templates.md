## How to Use This Doc

These templates are meant to be **customized, not blasted out unchanged**. The goal is to sound like a thoughtful indie team with a clear hook, not a PR machine.

For Phase 3, the outreach goal is simple:
- get **wishlist-driving coverage**
- secure **preview interest and creator replies**
- build awareness around Emotion Cards' core hook: **a roguelike deckbuilder where emotions shape both strategy and story**

Keep emails short, specific, and easy to skim.

---

## Core Positioning

Use some version of these points across outreach:
- **Genre hook:** roguelike deckbuilder
- **Differentiator:** emotions, memories, and emotional states drive card play
- **Audience fit:** deckbuilder players, roguelike fans, narrative indie players, cozy/reflective strategy audiences
- **Why it stands out:** strategic card play with emotional narrative weight
- **CTA:** wishlist, preview, demo/build interest, interview, or coverage consideration

### Short Internal Pitch

> Emotion Cards is a roguelike deckbuilder where your deck is built from emotions, memories, and emotional states — blending strategic card play with a more personal, story-driven arc.

### Optional Supporting Angles

Use only what fits the recipient:
- “for players who want deckbuilding with more narrative meaning”
- “a softer, more introspective twist on the roguelike deckbuilder space”
- “strategy driven by emotional synergy, not just damage math”
- “a deckbuilder aimed at players who love both systems and story”

## Press Outreach Email Template

**Best for:** journalists, editors, indie games writers, preview desks, newsletters, websites covering new releases or promising indies

**Subject line options:**
- Emotion Cards — press intro for a narrative roguelike deckbuilder
- Press pitch: Emotion Cards
- Announcement pitch: Emotion Cards from NorthernStar Studio
- An emotional twist on the roguelike deckbuilder formula

### Template

```text
Subject: [Choose one of the subject lines above]

Hi [Name],

I’m [Your Name] from NorthernStar Studio. I wanted to share **Emotion Cards**, an upcoming roguelike deckbuilder where cards represent emotions, memories, and emotional states.

The core pitch is straightforward: we’re building a deckbuilder that aims to pair strong run-based strategy with a more personal narrative layer, so choices feel meaningful both mechanically and emotionally.

I thought it may be relevant for you because of your coverage of [specific article / beat / game / genre trend].

A few quick points:

- **Hook:** emotion-driven cards and emotional-state synergies
- **Angle:** a story-forward take on a genre often focused almost entirely on combat efficiency
- **Audience:** players who like deckbuilders, roguelikes, and narrative indie games

If helpful, I can send:
- screenshots and logo assets
- a concise fact sheet
- trailer / announcement materials
- build or demo information [if available]
- interview availability from the team

If this sounds like a fit for coverage, I’d be glad to send over the full press kit.

Best,
[Your Name]
NorthernStar Studio
[Email]
[Website / Steam page / press kit link]
```

### Notes

For press, the key is:
- lead with the hook fast
- show why it fits *their beat*
- avoid sounding like a press release pasted into an email
- give them everything needed to decide in under 20 seconds

If the outlet covers trends or business angles, add one line such as:

> We think it speaks to a clear appetite for deckbuilders with stronger narrative identity, not just mechanical novelty.

### Optional Update Add-On

If there is a real update, add **one** of these:
- “We’ve just published new screenshots/trailer assets.”
- “We now have updated preview materials available.”
- “We’re currently scheduling preview conversations for this phase.”
- “We’ve narrowed the pitch and thought this specific angle might suit your audience better.”

### Follow-Up Rule

Send **one follow-up** by default. Don’t keep nudging unless:
- they replied and asked for timing
- a major milestone changed the story
- they explicitly invited future updates

## Customization Notes

Every outreach message should be customized in at least 3 places:

### 1) Why them
Mention one real thing:
- a game they covered
- a format they do well (first-look, strategy breakdowns, indie spotlights)
- a recurring interest (deckbuilders, cozy indies, emotional/narrative games)

Examples:
- “I thought of your channel because you regularly cover deckbuilders with unusual hooks.”
- “Your coverage of narrative-forward indie games made me think this might land.”
- “You seem to have a good eye for strategy games with a strong thematic identity.”

### 2) Which angle fits
Don’t send the same pitch to everyone.

**For deckbuilder creators:**
- emphasize mechanics, synergies, run variety, tactical identity

**For narrative or cozy creators:**
- emphasize emotional framing, character arcs, reflective tone

**For press:**
- emphasize the market/category angle and what makes the game newsworthy

### 3) What you want from them
Be specific:
- wishlist consideration
- preview coverage
- receiving a build
- interview interest
- inclusion in a roundup

If the CTA is vague, replies drop.

## Tone Guidance

### What the tone should feel like
- confident, not loud
- thoughtful, not precious
- human, not automated
- informed, not over-rehearsed

### What to avoid
- “revolutionary” / “groundbreaking” / “genre-defining” language
- fake urgency
- exaggerated claims without proof
- overexplaining lore
- giant paragraphs
- obvious mass-email phrasing

### Good phrasing patterns
- “thought this might be a fit because…”
- “the core pitch is straightforward…”
- “if helpful, I can send…”
- “happy to share a short summary / assets / build details…”

### Phrasing to avoid
- “I hope this email finds you well”
- “I’m excited to announce”
- “the next big indie hit”
- “a unique and innovative experience”
- “just bumping this to the top of your inbox”
