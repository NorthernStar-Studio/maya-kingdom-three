# Emotion Cards — Creator Outreach List

**Owner:** Gabe  
**Branch:** `release/v0.4.0`  
**Focus:** Phase 3 creator outreach for a **narrative-forward roguelike deckbuilder** with indie strategy appeal.  
**Positioning hook:** “Slay the Spire-style replayability, but with emotional narrative choices and character-driven runs.”

## How to Use This List

- **Priority order:** Start with Tier 1 for wishlist/awareness spikes, then fill coverage density with Tiers 2-3.
- **Best pitch angle:** Lead with the game’s clear hook: **emotion-based deckbuilding + narrative consequences**.
- **Best asset package:** short trailer, polished key art, 3-5 GIFs, concise Steam description, and a creator key email with genre comps.
- **Suggested outreach window:**
  - **Wave A:** 6-8 weeks before demo/major public beat
  - **Wave B:** 10-14 days before demo or festival beat
  - **Wave C:** embargo lift / release week
  - **Wave D:** 2-4 weeks after launch for “hidden gem” and update coverage

## Pitch Angle by Creator Type

### For roguelike/deckbuilder creators
Lead with:
- build variety
- replayability
- card synergy hooks
- what makes runs feel different
- why it is **not just another Slay the Spire clone**

**Short angle:**
> Emotion Cards is a narrative-forward roguelike deckbuilder where your deck reflects feelings, memories, and emotional states. Runs still deliver card synergy and tough decisions, but those choices also shape character outcomes.

### For narrative/story creators
Lead with:
- emotional hook
- character arc framing
- meaningful choices
- how card play changes story outcomes
- “deckbuilder for players who want story payoffs”

**Short angle:**
> Emotion Cards turns emotional conflict into deckbuilding. Each run is a character journey where your choices in and out of combat shape the story, relationships, and ending.

### For indie discovery channels
Lead with:
- clear one-line hook
- strong visuals/GIFs
- Steam page + demo timing
- audience comps: **Slay the Spire**, **Griftlands**, **Monster Train**, with a more emotional narrative core

## Notes / Filters

- **Best overall fit:** creators who already cover **Slay the Spire-likes, Balatro-likes, roguelites, and thoughtful indie systems games**.
- **Most promising response-rate tier:** mid-size genre specialists like **Time to Grind**, **NeverNathaniel**, **Cobalt Streak**, and **I Dream of Indie Games**.
- **Narrative expansion lane:** **PlayFrame**, **Materwelonz**, **ChristopherOdd**, **Keith Ballard**, and **Mapocolops** can help Emotion Cards avoid being framed as “just another deckbuilder.”
- **Do not use the same email for everyone.** Split messaging into at least three variants: mechanics-first, narrative-first, and indie-discovery.
- **If only one build beat is available, prioritize demo/preview coverage** over cold launch-week key blasts.
