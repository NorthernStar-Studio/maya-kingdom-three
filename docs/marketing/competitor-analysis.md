## Detailed Competitor Profiles

### 1. Civilization VII (Firaxis Games)

**Overview:**
- Flagship historical strategy franchise
- Seventh main installment
- Expected 2026 release

**Strengths:**
- AAA production quality
- Deep strategic mechanics refined over decades
- Strong brand recognition
- Extensive mod support
- Large existing player base

**Weaknesses:**
- High price point ($70)
- Complex for casual players
- Annual release cycle fatigue
- Generic approach to civilizations

**Our Differentiation:**
- Focus on narrative depth and unique faction mechanics
- Trilogy conclusion appeal (players who've followed MK1 & MK2)
- Lower price point ($40-50)
- Cross-platform (including Switch 2)
- Specific Mesoamerican focus vs. generic global

---

### 2. Humankind (Amplitude Studios)

**Overview:**
- Historical 4X game with culture blending mechanics
- Released 2021
- Mixed reception but dedicated following

**Strengths:**
- Innovative "culture blending" system
- Beautiful art direction
- Strong historical accuracy
- Good replayability

**Weaknesses:**
- Mixed launch reception (bugs, balance)
- AI issues reported
- Less narrative depth than competitors
- Smaller developer, slower support

**Our Differentiation:**
- More polished at launch (learning from others' mistakes)
- Stronger narrative focus
- Specific Mesoamerican focus
- Better platform support (Switch 2 launch)

---

### 3. Old World (Mohawk Games)

**Overview:**
- Narrative-driven historical strategy
- Released 2021
- Cult following, high praise for writing

**Strengths:**
- Exceptional writing and narrative
- Unique "legacy" system (characters age/die)
- Deep diplomacy
- Strong replayability

**Weaknesses:**
- Niche audience appeal
- Smaller scale (fewer civilizations)
- Limited platform support (PC only)
- Higher learning curve

**Our Differentiation:**
- Broader platform reach
- Trilogy story (players invested in MK1 & MK2)
- More accessible to casual strategy fans
- Lower price point

---

### 4. Ara: History Untold (Oxide Games)

**Overview:**
- Historical 4X from creators of Ashes of the Singularity
- Released 2024
- Newer entrant to market

**Strengths:**
- Strong technical foundation
- Good graphics
- Innovative systems

**Weaknesses:**
- New IP, no brand recognition
- Limited content at launch
- Smaller player base

**Our Differentiation:**
- Established franchise (Maya Kingdom trilogy)
- Specific Mesoamerican focus
- Stronger marketing (learning from MK1 & MK2)

## Key Takeaways

### What We Need to Do

1. **Emphasize Narrative** — Old World proves narrative sells in this genre
2. **Polish at Launch** — Humankind's issues show launch quality matters
3. **Cross-Platform** — Only major competitor is Civ VII; we can reach Switch 2 players
4. **Price Point** — $40-50 range is sweet spot vs. $70 Civ VII
5. ** trilogy Hook** — Leverage existing MK1 & MK2 players

### Success Factors

- Strong launch (avoid Humankind launch issues)
- Clear Mesoamerican focus (differentiation)
- Good story/narrative (compete with Old World)
- Cross-platform reach (PC + Switch 2)
- Competitive pricing
