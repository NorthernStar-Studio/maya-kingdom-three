# Aztec Civilization Mechanics

## Overview

The Aztec civilization represents a warrior society built on honor, sacrifice, and the cosmic battle between gods. In Maya Kingdom Three, they excel at aggressive early-game expansion and combat bonuses when cornered.

**Leader:** Moctezuma II - The Great Speaker  
**Civilization Trait:** Flower Wars - strength in adversity

---

## Leader: Moctezuma II - The Great Speaker

### Leader Bonus
**Title of the Fifth Sun:** All military units gain +15% combat strength when below 50% health. This bonus stacks with the Flower Wars civilization ability.

### Leader Unique Ability
**Tribute Network:** Aztecs generate +1 Gold per military unit on the map. Bonus increases to +2 Gold per unit at Political Civic.

---

## Special Ability: Flower Wars (Civilization Trait)

**Flower Wars (Pixtlācatl):** When any Aztec unit has less than 50% health, it gains +20% combat strength and +1 movement.

This reflects the Aztec tradition of capturing enemies for ritual sacrifice - wounded warriors fight with desperate ferocity, believing their deaths in battle ensure cosmic balance.

**Synergy:** Combines powerfully with Moctezuma's leader bonus for a cumulative +35% combat strength when wounded.

---

## Start Bonus

**Temple Foundation:** Aztecs start the game with a Temple already constructed in their capital.

- Immediate religious production capability
- Can train Jaguar Warriors from turn 1
- +2 Faith per turn baseline
- Unlocks Priest unit immediately

### Aqueduct (Acueducto)
Aztecs receive Aqueduct technology earlier (+1 Water capacity) and Floating Gardens provide +1 additional Food.

## Victory Conditions Emphasis

- **Domination:** Strong early-game military with Flower Wars combat bonus
- **Religious:** Temple start provides Faith advantage; Priests can spread influence
- **Cultural:** Eagle Knight captures provide era score; unique buildings add flavor

---

## Strategy Notes

1. **Early Aggression:** Start with Temple enables immediate Jaguar Warrior production. Push hard in early eras.

2. **Wound Tactics:** Intentionally accepting combat at sub-50% health triggers Flower Wars + Moctezuma bonuses. Consider positioning units to take damage from terrain before committing.

3. **Resource Synergy:** Build Floating Gardens near any water tiles. Combine with Cacao/Sugar resources for amplified production.

4. **Economic Warfare:** Moctezuma's Tribute Network generates significant Gold through unit count. Maintain large armies even during peace to fund later conquest.

---

## Historical Notes

The Aztecs (Mexica) founded their capital Tenochtitlan in 1325 on a lake island, growing into a powerful empire through military conquest and tributary relationships. Their "Flower Wars" (Xochiyaoyotl) were ritualized conflicts designed to capture prisoners for sacrifice rather than purely territorial conquest - a unique military tradition that directly inspired the civilization's gameplay mechanic.

Moctezuma II (1466-1520) became emperor in 1502, ruling at the height of Aztec power before encountering Spanish conquistadors. His title "The Great Speaker" (Ilhuicatlatenco) reflected the divine authority of the Aztec ruler.
